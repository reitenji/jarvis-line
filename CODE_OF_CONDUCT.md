# Code of Conduct

Jarvis Line welcomes respectful participation from users and contributors of every background and experience level.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and constructive.
- Critique ideas and code without attacking people.
- Respect privacy and avoid sharing another person's logs, credentials, or personal information.
- Accept correction gracefully and help keep discussions useful to the project.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, deliberate intimidation, doxxing, repeated disruption, and publishing private information are not accepted in project spaces.

## Scope

This policy applies to repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, release conversations, and other spaces where someone represents the Jarvis Line project.

## Reporting and Enforcement

Do not post confidential conduct reports publicly. Use [GitHub's private abuse-reporting tools](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse) and include the repository URL plus links to the relevant content. Until the project publishes a dedicated confidential maintainer contact, this is the private reporting path for conduct and safety concerns.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close conversations, reject contributions, or temporarily or permanently restrict participation when this policy is violated. Conduct concerns that become known to maintainers will be reviewed in good faith with attention to the safety and privacy of everyone involved.
